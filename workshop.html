Post Tension Workshop

Registration

  • Open for individual participation.

Objective

This workshop focuses on delivering an in-depth understanding of the Post-Tensioning System, an advanced prestressing technique widely used in modern structural engineering. The session is designed to strengthen conceptual clarity and technical insight into high-performance concrete systems.

Session Highlights

  • Fundamentals of prestressed concrete
  • Principle of post-tensioning and induced compressive stresses
  • Stress distribution and load transfer mechanism
  • Components: high-tensile steel tendons, anchor heads, wedges, and ducts
  • Flexural behavior and deflection control
  • Crack mitigation and serviceability performance
  • Comparison between conventional RCC and post-tensioned structures
  • Applications in long-span slabs, flat slabs, transfer beams, bridge decks, and high-rise buildings
  • Structural efficiency, durability, and economic advantages

Duration

  • The workshop will be conducted for 45 minutes to 1 hour.

Participation Guidelines

  • Participants must maintain professional discipline throughout the session.
  • Active engagement during the technical discussion segment is encouraged.
  • Mobile devices should be kept on silent mode.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the mechanics of pre-compression in concrete
  • Gain insight into structural optimization using post-tension systems
  • Develop technical knowledge relevant to design considerations and construction practices
  • Enhance conceptual understanding of advanced concrete technology

Certification

  • Participation certificates will be provided to all attendees.

Note

  • Instructions provided during the session must be strictly followed.
